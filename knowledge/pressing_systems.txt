GEGENPRESSING (Counter-Pressing)
Pioneered by Jürgen Klopp at Borussia Dortmund and perfected at Liverpool.
Core principle: immediately after losing possession, the entire team presses
as a coordinated unit to win the ball back within 5-8 seconds.
The logic: the opponent is most disorganized in the first seconds after
receiving the ball. Pressing immediately exploits that disorganization.

Key requirements for gegenpressing to work:
- High team fitness (requires intense preseason conditioning)
- Clear pressing triggers: back passes, poor first touches, wide passes to fullbacks
- Compact shape when out of possession — players must be within pressing distance
- The striker presses to cut off the pass to the holding midfielder, not to win the ball directly

Klopp's Liverpool shape when pressing: 4-3-3 compresses to a 4-2-4 or 4-1-5
with both wingers pressing high alongside the striker...

HIGH PRESS vs. MID-BLOCK
High press: team's defensive line sits at or above the halfway line.
Forces opponents to play long balls or make mistakes under pressure.
Requires: high defensive line, offside trap discipline, fast center-backs.
Risk: space in behind for fast strikers to exploit.

Mid-block: team sits between their own half and the midfield line.
Absorbs pressure, defends compactly, looks to counter on transitions.
Used by: Atletico Madrid under Simeone, many mid-table Premier League sides.
Trade-off: less pressure on the ball, but harder to break down...
